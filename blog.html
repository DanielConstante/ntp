What is a “palapa”? A palapa is simply the Spanish word for a shaded, open area using palm leaves as a roof, similar to a “tiki hut.” The Aztecs and Mayans originally used this word to describe their houses. Palapas are popular in southern Mexico, Central America, and are well-known among most tropical countries, beaches, and resorts around the world. They provide excellent shade and are a fresh retreat from intense heat. The price for each type of palapa varies, depending on square footage of the structure and any specific customizations made. What materials are used to make a palapa? At North Texas Palapas, we use the finest materials: all natural red cedar wood, authentic palm leaves or synthetic palms, and rope for a rustic finish. Synthetic palms are more durable over the elements and are guaranteed to last 20+ years. Natural palm leaves will last 7-8 years before needing to be replaced, and the sturdy cedar frame in our palapas has a 10 year warranty.
